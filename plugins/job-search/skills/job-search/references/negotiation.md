## 1. Salary Research

### Web Search Patterns
- **Glassdoor** - Company reviews + salary ranges (search "Company + Salary + Role")
- **AmbitionBox** - India-focused, CTC breakdowns, interview insights
- **Levels.fyi** - Tech roles, detailed leveling, company-specific bands
- **Blind** - Anonymous posts, real offers (filter by company/location)
- **PayScale** - Adjustable for location, experience, education

### Indian CTC Breakdown
Understand each component when comparing offers:
- **Base Salary** - Fixed monthly pay
- **HRA** - House Rent Allowance (typically 10-30% of base)
- **Special Allowance** - Taxable component
- **PF Contribution** - Employer contribution (12%)
- **Variable Pay** - Bonus, performance incentive (often capped in first year)
- **Stock** - ESOP vesting (4-year cliff common)
- **Other** - LTA, medical, conveyance

**Total CTC â‰  Take-home Pay** - Account for taxes, PF deductions.

### US/Global Structure
- **Base** - Fixed annual salary
- **Equity** - RSUs/stock options (4-year vesting, 1-year cliff typical)
- **Bonus** - Target % of base (50-200% common in tech)
- **Benefits** - 401k match, health insurance, PTO, relocation
- **Sign-on Bonus** - One-time cash (negotiate separately)

### Adjustment Factors
- Location multiplier (SF Bay > Austin > Mid-West)
- Company stage (startup < mid-size < FAANG)
- Experience level (IC1-7 bands vary 2-5x)
- In-demand skills (AI/ML, DevOps premium; QA lower)
- Unemployment/employment status

## 3. Negotiation Strategies

### Anchor High (15-20% Above Target)
- Research range beforehand; ask 15-20% above midpoint
- They expect negotiation; anchoring high creates room
- Example: Target â‚¹25L CTC â†’ Ask â‚¹29-30L

### Total Comp Reframe
If base is capped, negotiate the full package:
- "What flexibility exists on equity/signing bonus/PTO/stock options?"
- Equity vesting schedules (push cliff from 1y to 0.5y if possible)
- Sign-on bonus helps offset current job loss

### Competing Offer Play
- "I have another offer, and I prefer your company. What can you improve?"
- Only mention if genuine; never fabricate
- Show interest first, then mention offer; reverses power dynamic

### Delay Tactic
- Request 48-72 hours to review (buying time for other offers)
- "I need to discuss with family/advisor before committing"
- Deadlines often extend if you're top choice

### Creative Package Approach
If cash is locked:
- Remote work days (WFH 3-4 days/week)

- Conference attendance
- Accelerated review cycle (6-month review instead of 12)

## 6. Accepting / Declining

**Accepting:**
Get written confirmation before resigning:
"Thank you for the revised offer. I accept the position of [Role] with [Company] at [CTC/package]. Please send written confirmation."

**Declining Professionally:**
"Thank you for the opportunity. I'm moving forward with another offer that aligns better with my current priorities. I appreciate your time."
*Keep door open for future.*

**Resigning Current Job:**
- Provide written resignation notice (meeting + email)
- Serve full notice period (honor commitments)
- Document knowledge transfer
- Don't badmouth company; maintain relationships

## 7. Indian-Specific Considerations

**CTC Negotiation:**
- Most negotiable: base + variable (guarantee % for yr 1)
- Less negotiable: HRA (set by policy)
- Question: "What's the variable pay guarantee in year 1?"

**Variable Pay:**
- Negotiate guarantee percentage (e.g., 50% guaranteed, 50% performance-based)
- Clarify measurement criteria (individual goals vs team vs company)

**Notice Period Buyout:**
- Standard: 1-2 months buyout fee common
- Negotiate: "Can you pay 3 months salary to exit immediately?"
- Timing: Buyout often absorbed by new company HR

**Gratuity & PF:**
