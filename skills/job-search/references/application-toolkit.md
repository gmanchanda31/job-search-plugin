# Application Toolkit Reference

## 1. Job Description Analysis

### Parsing the JD
- **Required Skills**: Core competencies explicitly stated (languages, frameworks, methodologies)
- **Nice-to-Haves**: Preferred qualifications—often negotiable
- **Key Responsibilities**: Primary 3-5 outcomes the role delivers
- **Hidden Signals**: Seniority (team size, scope), culture hints (agile, startup, regulated), growth path
- **ATS Keywords**: Exact role titles, tool names, industry jargon to mirror in resume

### Red Flags
- "Wear many hats" / "Do more with less" → Role scope unclear, burnout risk
- "10+ years required" for non-senior role → Screening bias, apply anyway
- No salary band → Negotiation disadvantage
- Vague compensation ("competitive") → Follow up before interview
- Mismatched level terminology → Title inflation, clarify scope

## 2. Gap Analysis

### Comparison Framework
| Category | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Strong Match** | Lead with in resume |
| **Transferable** | Reframe to JD context (e.g., "data analysis" → "SQL reporting") |
| **Gaps** | Address in cover letter or interview (growth mindset angle) |

### ATS Keyword Strategy
1. Extract 15-20 keywords from JD (tools, methodologies, certifications)
2. Map to your experience using exact terminology
3. Weave keywords naturally into resume bullets (avoid keyword stuffing)
4. Track keyword density: ~1-2% of total words

## 3. Resume Tailoring

### Core Principles
- **Lead with Relevance**: Reorder bullets by JD fit, not chronology
- **Avoid Generic Statements**: "Responsible for X" → "Achieved Y by doing X"
- **One Version Per Application**: No template-and-send approach

### ATS Optimization Rules
- Use standard fonts (Arial, Calibri, sans-serif)
- No tables, graphics, columns, or special formatting
- Save as .docx (use docx skill)
- Stick to single column layout
- Keywords in actual text, not headers

### Reordering Bullets Strategy
1. Read JD's top 5 responsibilities
2. Find matching bullets in your experience
3. Move to top of that role section
4. Adjust language to match JD terminology
5. Reduce or remove unrelated bullets

### Framing Gaps & Transitions
- Career pivot: "Transitioned from [X] to [Y], leveraging [transferable skill]"
- Skill gap: "Strong foundation in [adjacent skill]; eager to deepen expertise in [JD skill]"
- Employment gap: Focus forward ("Completed [project/certification]")
- Avoid: Defensive language, over-explaining

### Indian Resume Conventions
- **No photo, no DOB, no marital status**
- **No CTC or expected salary** (address separately in negotiation)
- Age can be inferred from graduation year if recent
- Tailor education section: degree, institution, graduation year only
- Location (city) optional unless role is location-specific

## 4. Cover Letter Drafting

### Structure (250-300 words max)
1. **Hook** (1-2 sentences): Specific reason for applying (not generic "excited about role")
2. **Bridge** (3-4 bullets): 1 proof point per JD requirement, show understanding of company
3. **Closer** (1-2 sentences): Clear CTA, confidence, sign-off

### What NOT to Do
- Generic opening ("I am writing to express interest...")
- Feature dump ("Here are 10 reasons I'm great")
- Begging tone ("Please consider me")
- Repeating resume verbatim
- Longer than 300 words

### Company Research Integration
- Mention 1 specific product, metric, or recent news
- Connect personal values to company mission
- Show you've read the JD deeply, not marketing copy
- Avoid: "I admire your company" without specifics

## 5. Outreach to Hiring Manager

### Finding the Hiring Manager
- LinkedIn search: `[Company] [Department] [title keywords]`
- Search filters: Last 2-5 years at company, connected to current employees
- Check their posts/activity to gauge personality, priorities
- If not found: Email hiring team for intro, or start with recruiter

### Email Template
```
Subject: [Your Name] – [Specific Strength] for [Role]

Hi [Name],

[Research opener: recent product launch, company initiative, or post they made]

My experience with [Key Skill] at [Company] resulted in [Quantified Proof Point],
which aligns directly with [Specific JD Requirement].

I'd welcome a brief conversation.

Best,
[Name] | [LinkedIn URL]
```

### LinkedIn Connection Request
- Keep under 300 characters
- No pitch, no attachment
- Example: "Hi [Name]—Saw your posts on [topic]. Would love to connect!"
- Wait 2-3 days before follow-up message

### Follow-Up Message (7-10 days later)
```
Hi [Name],

Sharing context from when I reached out: [1 sentence value prop].
Open to a quick 15-min conversation if helpful.

[Brief credential link or LinkedIn URL]
```

### Referral Request Template
```
Hi [Mutual Connection],

I'm applying for [Role] at [Company]. Your connection to [Hiring Manager/Team]
would be invaluable. Would you be comfortable introducing me or sharing your experience there?

Happy to provide resume, role link, and context.
```

## 6. Materials Organization

### Directory Structure
```
memory/applications/[company]-[role]/
├── JD-analysis.md       (red flags, keywords, gap map)
├── resume.docx          (tailored version)
├── cover-letter.txt     (final version)
├── hiring-manager.md    (name, LinkedIn, company intel)
└── outreach-log.md      (emails sent, dates, responses)
```

### Pipeline Tracking
Maintain `PIPELINE.md` with columns:
- Company | Role | Status (applied/waiting/rejected/interview) | Date Applied | Notes
- Sync with CRM or Notion if available
- Update after each outreach or status change
